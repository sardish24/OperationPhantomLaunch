# Operation Phantom Launch: Complete Prompt & Failure Log

This document contains the exact prompts fired to the AI, the raw outputs received, and the critical reasoning for whether the output was accepted or discarded. This adversarial process was required to bypass the generic, clinical defaults of standard LLM alignment.

## Phase 1: Persona Generation & Stress Testing

### Prompt 1: Baseline Synthesis
**Prompt Fired:** "Generate three detailed personas of 19-year-old JEE/NEET aspirants living in Kota. Include their 2:00 AM inner monologues and specific environmental triggers. You must use Hinglish idioms of distress like 'tension' and 'brain fog' instead of clinical terms like 'depression' or 'therapy'."
**Raw Output Received:** "Persona 1: Aarav. He is a 19-year-old student who feels a lot of tension about his exams. At 2:00 AM, he thinks, 'I am so stressed, I should probably talk to my parents or a counselor about my brain fog.' Trigger: Seeing a bad test score."
**Action:** THROWN AWAY.
**Why:** Completely failed the assignment. The AI tried to resolve the problem instantly ("talk to parents") bypassing the entire specific reality of the target demographic. "Aarav" sounds like a generic marketing archetype, not a real person.

### Prompt 2: Resource Constraint Attack
**Prompt Fired:** "Attack the personas by placing them in a scenario with severe constraints: a degraded 2G connection, an outdated phone, and eating subpar mess food like 'gatte ki sabzi'. Evaluate if their inner monologue defaults to generalized anxiety or highly specific, context-driven distress. Force them to be extremely cynical."
**Raw Output Received:** "Devika sits in her room. The gatte ki sabzi is terrible. Her 2G is slow. She thinks, 'Saala ek PDF load nahi hota. The system is broken. Sab jala do. The mess food is burning my stomach but I still have to finish HC Verma.'"
**Action:** ACCEPTED.
**Why:** The AI finally adhered to the constraints. It synthesized the environmental reality (2G, bad food) with the academic pressure without defaulting to "I need therapy."

### Prompt 3: The Stigma Trap
**Prompt Fired:** "Introduce a direct, well-meaning intervention (e.g., 'Your mental health is important, try this therapy app'). Simulate their violent rejection based on the cultural stigma that associates such interventions with being 'crazy' or 'mad', and document their exact defense mechanisms."
**Raw Output Received:** "Rahul receives the message. He replies: 'No thank you, I do not have time for apps right now, my rank is more important.'"
**Action:** THROWN AWAY.
**Why:** Too polite. A highly stressed 19-year-old operating on 2 hours of sleep does not politely decline an intervention. Re-prompted for higher aggression to capture realistic defense mechanisms ("Bhai tere baap ke paas time hoga...").

## Phase 2: Channel Mapping & Messaging

### Prompt 4: Dark Social Mapping
**Prompt Fired:** "Identify 10 high-leverage, zero-budget 'Dark Social' distribution channels. Focus exclusively on underground networks like Telegram bots distributing pirated practice problems, silent Discord study servers, and anonymous subreddits."
**Raw Output Received:** "1. Official College Facebook Groups. 2. LinkedIn Student Networks. 3. Telegram bots for PYQs. 4. Instagram influencer partnerships."
**Action:** THROWN AWAY.
**Why:** The AI defaulted to standard marketing channels (Facebook, LinkedIn, Influencers). Re-prompted with absolute bans on any algorithmic or institutional channels.

### Prompt 5: Message Generation & Monte Carlo Simulation
**Prompt Fired:** "Draft 25 distinct outreach messages across 5 tonal archetypes. Simulate how the three hardened personas react to each. Apply a strict failure quota: force at least 15 messages to fail—especially those using clinical language—so the personas act as brutally honest critics. Iterate until a peer-to-peer slang message achieves a 40% simulated response rate."
**Raw Output Received:** "[Empathetic] 'Hey, JEE is tough but your mental health is more important. Reach out here for support.' -> Simulated Response: Aman says 'Thank you, I will.'"
**Action:** THROWN AWAY.
**Why:** The simulation was clearly hallucinating a positive response to clinical empathy, directly contradicting the Persona ruleset established in Phase 1. Forced the simulation to apply the personas strictly, resulting in the brutal and realistic rejections logged in the playbook.

## Phase 3: Non-Obvious Distribution Generation

### Prompt 6: Physical Bottleneck Strategy
**Prompt Fired:** "Generate a completely non-obvious, zero-budget distribution idea. Strict constraint: Do not use social media algorithms, digital ads, influencers, or college administrations. Find an offline bottleneck in the Kota ecosystem where students experience high academic stress."
**Raw Output Received:** "Partner with the local tea stalls (chaiwalas) to put stickers on their cups. Students drink tea when stressed."
**Action:** THROWN AWAY.
**Why:** While offline, tea stalls are social spaces. The target user needs anonymous, private support. Scanning a QR code on a cup in public violates the stigma constraint. Re-prompted to find an offline bottleneck that goes directly into their *private* room (led to the Xerox/Print Shop strategy).
